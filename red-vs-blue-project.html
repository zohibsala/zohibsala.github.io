RED vs BLUE

Overview

RED vs BLUE is a short 3D animation created in Autodesk Maya. The project focuses on character animation and movement through a simple football scenario set inside a child's bedroom.

The animation follows two anthropomorphic crayons, a red and a blue character, competing in a short football moment where the red crayon attempts to score against the blue crayon. The goal was to create a clear and engaging sequence driven entirely by movement, timing and camera work.

My Role

I was responsible for the full production of the animation, including modelling, rigging, animation, lighting, camera setup, rendering and final editing.

Key Features

  • Short 46 second animated sequence with a clear beginning, middle and end
  • Character animation using keyframing techniques
  • Bone based rigging system for both crayon characters
  • Deformer based animation for the football to simulate realistic rotation
  • Multiple camera angles to create a cinematic presentation
  • Environment set in a detailed child's bedroom with various props
  • Use of sound effects and background music to enhance the final output

Technical Implementation

The project was created using Autodesk Maya 2024.1 for modelling, rigging and animation, with CapCut used for post production editing.

Both characters were rigged using a bone based system, allowing them to move with flexibility and perform actions such as running, jumping, diving and celebrating. The football was animated using a deformer to achieve realistic rotational movement during play.

The animation was built using keyframe techniques, with a strong focus on timing, anticipation and smooth transitions between actions. Multiple cameras were set up and animated to follow the action and highlight key moments in the scene.

Lighting and rendering required experimentation to achieve a balanced result. After testing different lighting setups, an ambient lighting approach was used to ensure the scene remained clear and visually consistent during rendering.

Challenges

One of the main challenges was achieving smooth and consistent keyframe timing. This required repeated adjustments and testing to ensure movements felt natural and not too fast or slow.

Camera movement was another challenge, as early attempts resulted in unstable or abrupt motion. This was improved through careful refinement of keyframes and camera paths.

Rendering also presented difficulties, including lighting issues and managing a large number of image frames during the export process. Additional challenges occurred during editing, where some playback inconsistencies had to be investigated and resolved.

Outcome

RED vs BLUE demonstrates my understanding of fundamental animation principles including timing, movement and character expression through motion.

The project strengthened my skills in Autodesk Maya, particularly in rigging, keyframe animation, camera work and rendering. It also gave me practical experience in managing a full animation pipeline from concept to final output.
