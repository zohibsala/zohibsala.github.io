Zohib Sala

3D Artist / Technical Artist

I am a final year Games Design and Development student at the University of Greenwich, graduating this July. I focus on gameplay programming, interactive systems and real time 3D environments using Unity, Unreal Engine, Maya and more.

I enjoy building playable experiences that feel responsive, polished and engaging. My work combines technical problem solving with visual creativity, allowing me to design systems while also shaping how a game looks and feels.

My Journey

My interest in game development grew naturally from playing games during my childhood. As I got older, I became more curious about how games are actually made. I often found myself thinking about how characters and environments are created, how they look so detailed and realistic, and what goes on behind the scenes to bring everything together.

That curiosity led me to study Games Design and Development at the University of Greenwich, where I began building my own projects and developing practical skills in both programming and 3D art.

During my degree, I have created multiple games and 3D environments, working with gameplay mechanics, animation systems, UI and level design. Projects such as my multiplayer Unity game and my Unreal Engine zombie shooter helped me understand how to take an idea from concept to a complete, playable experience.

Through these projects, I have learned how to structure systems, solve technical challenges and continuously improve performance and user experience. Each project has helped me become more efficient, more detail focused and more aware of how games are built in real production environments.

What I Focus On

Gameplay Programming

Designing and implementing mechanics such as player interaction, movement systems and game flow using C# in Unity and Blueprints in Unreal Engine.

3D Art and Environment Design

Creating models and environments in Maya and bringing them into real time engines with attention to lighting, composition and optimisation.

Polish and User Experience

I focus on making games feel smooth and complete through UI design, game feedback and overall presentation.

What Sets Me Apart

I do not just experiment with ideas. I finish projects and turn them into complete experiences that people can play.

I understand both the technical and creative sides of development, which allows me to work across gameplay systems and visual design without losing quality in either.

I am always looking to improve, whether that is writing cleaner code, building better environments, or learning new tools that make my work more efficient and professional.
