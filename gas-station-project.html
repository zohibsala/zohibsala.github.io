Gas Station

Overview

Gas Station Environment is a realistic 3D scene created in Autodesk Maya and Unreal Engine 5, presented as a real time environment. This project focuses specifically on the use of physically based rendering (PBR) materials to achieve a high level of visual realism and immersion.

The goal was to design a small, controlled environment that demonstrates how accurate material definition and lighting interaction can enhance the believability of a scene.

Inspiration and Research

The environment is inspired by modern urban settings commonly seen in games such as GTA V. Visual references were taken from real world gas stations, focusing on materials such as asphalt, concrete, metal, plastic and glass.

Research was also informed by physically based rendering workflows and real time rendering techniques, with particular attention to how surfaces respond to light. The aim was to recreate realistic material behaviour rather than just visual appearance.

My Role

I was responsible for the full production of the environment, including:

  • 3D modelling of all assets in Maya
  • Scene layout and environment composition
  • Creation and application of physically based materials
  • Lighting setup using Unreal Engine 5
  • Implementation of emissive and reflective materials
  • Exporting and presenting the scene in Unreal Engine
  • Basic optimisation for real time performance

Key Features

  • Fully walkable gas station environment
  • Use of physically based rendering (PBR) materials
  • Realistic surface detail including roughness, reflections and normal maps
  • Emissive materials for screens and lighting fixtures
  • Environmental storytelling through dirt, wear and surface imperfections
  • Consistent lighting using Unreal Engine 5's real time system
  • Real time exploration using a first person perspective

Technical Implementation

The environment was modelled in Autodesk Maya using polygon modelling techniques, starting from primitive shapes and refining them through extrusion, scaling and vertex manipulation.

A modular approach was used for assets such as fuel pumps, shelves and interior props, allowing for efficient reuse and consistent design across the environment.

The main focus of the implementation was on physically based materials. Each material was created using standard PBR workflows, including base colour, roughness, metallic and normal maps. These were applied based on the type of surface to ensure realistic interaction with lighting.

For example, asphalt surfaces include roughness variation to simulate wear, allowing light to scatter naturally across the surface. Concrete walls use normal maps to enhance depth without increasing geometry complexity. Reflective materials such as tiles, mirrors and counters were adjusted to respond accurately to light sources within the scene.

Emissive materials were applied to screens and lighting elements to simulate light emission, improving realism and adding visual interest to interior areas.

The environment was imported into Unreal Engine 5 using FBX files, where materials were refined and lighting was set up using the engine's real time rendering system. Care was taken to ensure that all materials behaved consistently under the same lighting conditions.

Challenges

One of the main challenges was finding high quality textures that matched the level of realism required for physically based rendering.

Even when suitable textures were found, additional work was needed to adjust roughness, metallic and normal values to ensure that materials looked realistic within the engine. Some materials initially appeared flat or unconvincing and required multiple iterations to achieve the desired result.

Other challenges included texture alignment issues after import and ensuring that all materials remained visually consistent across the environment.

Reflection and Improvements

This project highlights my ability to apply physically based rendering techniques to create a realistic environment.

While the overall scene achieves a strong level of realism, there are areas that could be improved, such as:

  • Refining material quality further for more consistency
  • Improving small surface details for added realism
  • Expanding the environment to include more variation
  • Further optimisation for performance

These reflections have helped improve my understanding of material workflows and real time rendering.

Outcome

This project demonstrates my ability to design and develop a complete 3D environment with a strong focus on material realism.

It shows my understanding of physically based rendering, lighting interaction and environment composition, as well as my ability to create a believable real time scene using industry standard tools.

The project highlights how detailed materials and accurate surface behaviour can significantly improve the overall quality and immersion of a 3D environment.
